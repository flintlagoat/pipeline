Costco has sold their hot dog and soda combo for a dollar fifty since 1985. That's forty years — and the price has never moved. Meanwhile everything around it has doubled, tripled, gone up five times. So how does a company that size just... not raise the price?
The answer is the hot dog isn't a product. It's a signal. It tells you the moment you walk in: this place is on your side. That feeling is worth more than whatever margin they'd recover on a fifty-cent price increase. Costco knows exactly what they're doing. The hot dog is the cheapest marketing they've ever bought.
